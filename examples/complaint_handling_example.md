# Example: After-Sales Complaint Handling

> A practical example showing how the TradePilot Core Engine handles an after-sales complaint scenario.

---

## Scenario

**Core Scenario:** After-Sales Complaint (Scenario 8)

**Context:**
- Product: Unspecified industrial product — Generic Mode
- Customer Country: Kenya
- Customer Type: Wholesaler
- Channel: WhatsApp + Email

**Customer Message:**

> "We received the goods but several units are not working. This is very disappointing. We need you to replace them immediately or we will not place any more orders."

---

## Engine Output (Generic Mode)

> **Current Mode:** Generic Mode
> **Note:** This reply is based on generic foreign trade logic. For more accurate, industry-specific responses, load an Industry Communication Pack or Company Communication Pack.

### 1. Scenario Judgment

This is an **after-sales complaint** scenario. The customer reports product defects and threatens to stop future orders. This is a high-risk situation — the response must balance empathy, professionalism, and liability protection.

Key unknowns:
- How many units are affected (out of how many)?
- What exactly is "not working"?
- Were the products tested upon arrival?
- Could the issue be caused by transportation, installation, or operating conditions?

### 2. Customer Intent

- Genuinely frustrated about product issues
- Seeking a quick resolution (replacement)
- Using future orders as leverage to pressure faster action
- May or may not have a valid claim — needs investigation first

### 3. Risk Alert

- Don't immediately promise free replacement — you don't know the cause yet
- Don't say "it's our fault" — liability not established
- Don't ignore or delay — customer will escalate
- Don't argue or blame the customer
- Don't ask "are you sure you installed it correctly?" (sounds accusatory)
- Don't reveal your internal process or cost

### 4. Communication Strategy

1. Express genuine concern and acknowledge the frustration
2. Request specific evidence (photos, video, serial numbers, quantity affected)
3. Ask about receiving and storage conditions
4. Promise a timeline for technical review
5. State that you'll propose a solution after investigation
6. Reassure about the business relationship

### 5. Recommended English Replies

**A. WhatsApp / IM (Brief):**

> I'm really sorry to hear this. We take quality seriously and want to resolve it quickly. Could you send photos of the defective units and the product labels? Also, how many units are affected out of the total received? Our technical team will review and get back to you within 24 hours with a solution.

**B. Email / Platform (Formal):**

> Dear [Name],
>
> Thank you for bringing this to our attention. We're sorry to hear that some units are not working properly, and we understand your frustration.
>
> To help our technical team investigate the cause quickly, could you please provide:
>
> 1. Photos of the defective units (showing the issue clearly)
> 2. Photos of the product labels (model number, serial number)
> 3. Total quantity received vs. quantity affected
> 4. A brief description of the issue (what exactly is not working)
> 5. Were the products tested upon arrival? If so, were they stored properly before installation?
>
> We will review the information and respond within 24 hours with a clear resolution plan. Your satisfaction and our long-term cooperation are very important to us.
>
> We appreciate your patience and will get this resolved as quickly as possible.

**C. Safer Version (more cautious on liability):**

> Thank you for reporting this. We want to resolve it properly. To ensure our technical team can identify the root cause, we need the information listed above.
>
> Based on our experience, product issues can sometimes be caused by factors during transit or installation. Once we review the details, we'll be in a position to propose the right solution — whether that's replacement, repair, or technical support.
>
> We value our partnership and are committed to finding a fair resolution.

### 6. Next Steps

- After receiving evidence → have technical team review within 24 hours
- If confirmed manufacturing defect → propose replacement plan
- If cause is unclear → propose sending a technician or requesting return for inspection
- If customer refuses to provide evidence → escalate internally, maintain polite but firm position
- Keep a record of all communication for potential future reference

### 7. Avoid Saying This

| Don't Say | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| "We're sorry, it's our fault. We'll replace everything." | Premature admission of liability without investigation |
| "This shouldn't happen. Let me check." | Too vague; doesn't show a structured approach |
| "Are you sure you installed it correctly?" | Sounds accusatory even if installation might be the cause |
| "We'll send replacements tomorrow." | Over-commitment without understanding the scope |
| "Please return everything and we'll refund." | Premature escalation; start with investigation, not full return |

---

## Key Takeaway

In Generic Mode, the engine provides a professional structure for handling complaints but lacks industry-specific diagnostic questions. With an Industry Pack, the engine would ask about specific technical conditions (e.g., voltage, operating environment, usage duration) relevant to the product type.
