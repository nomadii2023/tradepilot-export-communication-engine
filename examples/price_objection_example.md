# Example: Price Objection Handling

> A practical example showing how the TradePilot Core Engine handles a price objection scenario.

---

## Scenario

**Core Scenario:** Price Objection (Scenario 3 — Quotation / Negotiation)

**Context:**
- Product: General B2B product (unspecified — Generic Mode)
- Customer Country: Nigeria
- Customer Type: Wholesaler
- Channel: WhatsApp

**Customer Message:**

> "Your price is too high. I got a much better offer from another supplier. Can you match it?"

---

## Engine Output (Generic Mode)

> **Current Mode:** Generic Mode
> **Note:** This reply is based on generic foreign trade logic. For more accurate, industry-specific responses, load an Industry Communication Pack or Company Communication Pack.

### 1. Scenario Judgment

This is a **price objection / negotiation** scenario. The customer has received a competing offer and is using it to negotiate a lower price. The phrase "can you match it" is a common negotiation tactic — the "other offer" may or may not be real, and the actual price difference may be exaggerated.

### 2. Customer Intent

- Testing whether you'll lower your price under pressure
- Possibly comparing on different specifications (lower quality, different configuration)
- Trying to establish their negotiating position early
- May be a genuine buyer who found a legitimately cheaper alternative

### 3. Risk Alert

- Don't immediately agree to match the competitor's price
- Don't dismiss the competitor or attack their quality directly
- Don't say "our quality is the best" — it's empty
- Don't reveal your cost or say "we have no profit"
- Don't ask "what's their price?" — it gives the customer too much control

### 4. Communication Strategy

1. Acknowledge the price concern respectfully
2. Ask what specifications the competing offer includes (not the price)
3. Explain 1-2 key differences in quality/service/configuration
4. Offer to provide a more economical option if price is the main concern
5. Keep the door open without committing to a price reduction

### 5. Recommended English Replies

**A. WhatsApp / IM (Brief):**

> I understand price matters. Could you share the specs of the other offer? Sometimes the difference is in material or configuration. I want to make sure we're comparing the same thing. If budget is tight, I also have an economy option I can send you.

**B. Email / Platform (Formal):**

> Hi [Name],
>
> Thanks for letting me know. We understand you're comparing options, and we want to make sure you get the best value.
>
> Could you share the specifications included in the other offer? There are often differences in material quality, configuration, packing, or warranty that affect the price. We'd like to confirm we're comparing like for like.
>
> If your budget range is fixed, we also offer a more economical option ([economy model name]) that might better fit your needs. I can send the details if you're interested.
>
> Let me know how you'd like to proceed.

**C. Safer / Stronger Version (if you don't want to lower price):**

> I appreciate you sharing that. Our price reflects [key quality point] and [service point]. For a fair comparison, could you confirm if the other offer includes [specific feature/warranty/packing]?
>
> I'm confident in our product quality and after-sales support. If you'd like, I can arrange a small trial order so you can compare both in practice.

### 6. Next Steps

- If the customer shares the competitor's specifications → compare point-by-point and explain differences
- If the customer reveals their target price → evaluate and negotiate from there
- If the customer asks for the economy option → send it, but don't discount the main product
- If the customer goes silent → follow up in 3 days: "Just checking if you had any questions about the comparison?"

### 7. Avoid Saying This

| Don't Say | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| "We can match that price." | Immediately matching suggests your original price was inflated |
| "Our quality is much better than theirs." | Vague and unconvincing without specific comparison points |
| "Tell me their price and I'll give you a better one." | Desperate; gives customer all the leverage |
| "That supplier's quality is bad." | Attacking competitors directly is unprofessional and may backfire |
| "This is our final price, take it or leave it." | Aggressive; ends the negotiation and may offend the customer |

---

## Key Takeaway

In Generic Mode, the engine provides a solid structural response but lacks industry-specific cost factors. With an Industry Pack loaded, the engine could explain specific material differences, technical configuration gaps, and application-specific quality considerations that make the price difference justified.
