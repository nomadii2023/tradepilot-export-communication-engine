## 3. Inquiry Reply Rules

### 3.1 Key Questions to Ask

For general trading, when a customer inquires, ask these before quoting:

1. **Product specifications** — exact model, size, material, color, or configuration
2. **Quantity** — how many units, total order value estimate
3. **Destination** — country and city (for shipping cost calculation)
4. **Timeline** — when do they need the goods
5. **Application** — what is the product used for (helps with recommendation)

### 3.2 Inquiry Reply Template

> Thank you for your inquiry. To prepare an accurate quotation, could you please confirm:
>
> 1. Exact product specifications (model, size, material, color)
> 2. Required quantity
> 3. Destination port or city
> 4. Required delivery date
> 5. Any specific certification or compliance requirements
>
> Once we have these details, we will send the quotation within [X hours/days].

### 3.3 Common Mistakes

- Don't send a full price list when the customer asks about one product
- Don't quote without confirming specifications
- Don't assume the customer knows trade terms (FOB, CIF, EXW)

## 4. Product Recommendation Logic

### 4.1 General Recommendation Principles

- Recommend based on the customer's described application
- Offer 2-3 options at different price points
- Explain the difference between options clearly
- Don't always recommend the most expensive

### 4.2 Recommendation Template

> Based on your requirement for [application], we recommend the following options:
>
> **Option A: [Model/Product Name]**
> - [Key spec 1]
> - [Key spec 2]
> - Price: [FOB $X/unit]
> - Suitable for: [scenario]
>
> **Option B: [Model/Product Name]**
> - [Key spec 1]
> - [Key spec 2]
> - Price: [FOB $X/unit]
> - Suitable for: [scenario]
>
> Would you like more details on either option?

## 5. Price Objection Handling

### 5.1 General Cost Factors

When a customer says "too expensive" or "competitor is cheaper":

| Factor | How to Explain |
|--------|---------------|
| Material quality | "Our product uses [material grade] vs. [lower grade] commonly used in cheaper alternatives" |
| Manufacturing process | "We use [process] which ensures [benefit]" |
| Packaging | "Our export packing includes [protection features] to reduce damage during shipping" |
| After-sales support | "We provide [warranty/support] which is not always included in lower-priced offers" |
| Certification | "Our product is certified [certification], which is required for [market]" |

### 5.2 Price Negotiation Template

> We understand your budget concern. Our price reflects:
> - [Quality point 1]
> - [Quality point 2]
> - [Service/support point]
>
> If you'd like to explore a more budget-friendly option, we also offer [economy model/product] at [price]. Would you like us to send the details?

## 6. After-Sales Troubleshooting

### 6.1 General Diagnostic Questions

When a customer reports an issue:

1. Could you send photos or a video of the problem?
2. What is the product model and serial number (from the label)?
3. When was the product received and when was it first used?
4. How was it installed / what conditions is it used in?
5. Have you tried [basic troubleshooting step]?

### 6.2 Complaint Response Template

> We're sorry to hear about the issue with [product]. We take quality seriously and want to resolve this quickly.
>
> Could you please send:
> 1. Photos of the product and the issue
> 2. Product label / serial number
> 3. Brief description of how and when the issue occurred
>
> Our technical team will review the information and get back to you within [X hours/days] with a solution.

## 7. DON'Ts — General Trading Rules

- Don't promise "best price" without knowing the customer's target
- Don't accept credit terms (O/A) for first-time customers
- Don't ship goods before receiving payment (or confirmed L/C)
- Don't over-commit on delivery timelines
- Don't ignore customer complaints — respond within 24 hours
- Don't make claims about certifications you don't hold
